===============================================================
RESEARCH GOALS - CLARIFIED
===============================================================

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTION:
  How long does it take for illnesses to manifest after
  environmental exposure?

OBJECTIVE:
  Identify lag periods (days) between environmental exposure
  and illness symptom onset for 3 different illness types

NOT THE GOAL:
  ✗ Maximize predictive performance (R², RMSE)
  ✗ Build production prediction model
  ✗ Optimize for deployment

THE ACTUAL GOAL:
  ✓ Understand which LAG PERIODS best explain illness
  ✓ Compare lag patterns across illness types
  ✓ Interpret lags as incubation/symptom onset periods
  ✓ Identify environmental factor-specific lag times

===============================================================
THREE TARGET ILLNESSES
===============================================================

1. Acute Upper Respiratory Infections
   → Acute laryngopharyngitis (dataset)
   Expected: SHORT lags (2-5 days)
   Status: ✓ TRAINED

2. Diseases of Esophagus, Stomach, Duodenum
   → Gastritis, unspecified (dataset)
   Expected: MEDIUM lags (3-10 days)
   Status: ⏳ TO TRAIN

3. Other Diseases of Upper Respiratory Tract
   → Chronic rhinitis (dataset)
   Expected: LONG lags (7-21 days, cumulative)
   Status: ⏳ TO TRAIN

===============================================================
KEY FINDINGS - ILLNESS #1 (Laryngopharyngitis)
===============================================================

Selected Lag Periods:
  • Temperature: 2 days
  • PM10 (air quality): 5 days
  • Humidity: 8 days
  • Humidity: 12 days
  • Temperature trend: 14 days

Average Lag: 8.2 days

Interpretation:
  ✓ Primary incubation: 2-5 days (rapid onset)
  ✓ Secondary effects: 8-12 days (cumulative/transmission)
  ✓ Different factors → different lag times
  ✓ Skipped 1, 3-4, 6-7 day lags (specific mechanisms)
